SSM Health Cardinal Glennon Congenital Syphilis Management Guidelines

Purpose of this website: This website is intended to function as a clinical support tool for clinicians who care for neonates where there is concern for congenital syphilis. The goal is to provide management recommendations based on the clinical information obtained from the birthing parent and neonate. Based on how the clinician answers the below questions, the algorithm will populate specific recommendations regarding further diagnostic testing, treatment and follow up. A rationale for these recommendations will also be provided. The goal of this clinical support tool is to provide recommendations on a wide range of inputted scenarios, including those that may not be fully addressed in the AAP Red Book.

Disclaimer: This website utilizes the reverse screening algorithm to provide recommendations and rationale regarding congenital syphilis management to help clinicians understand next steps when caring for a neonate at risk of congenital syphilis. These guidelines are not intended to replace clinical decision making, the AAP Red Book, and/or Infectious Diseases consultation.

•If the syphilitic stage is not mentioned in the medical record:
- IM benzathine penicillin G (Bicillin LA) once per week for 3 weeks in a row is sufficient treatment for essentially all stages of syphilis that we see in our birthing parent population. If more than 9 days elapsed between IM doses, the entire series should have been repeated. Consider this inadequate treatment if the series was not repeated.

• If the syphilitic stage is mentioned in the medical record or is clear based on the birthing parent's symptoms at the time:
- IM benzathine penicillin G (Bicillin LA) once per week for 3 weeks in a row is necessary for appropriate treatment of late latent syphilis and latent syphilis of unknown duration
- A single dose of IM benzathine penicillin G is sufficient treatment for primary, secondary, or early latent syphilis

•If the birthing parent was treated with a non-penicillin antibiotic during their pregnancy, this should be considered inadequate treatment.
•If the birthing parent was treated with an appropriate non-penicillin antibiotic (doxycycline, ceftriaxone) prior to pregnancy, this should be considered sufficient treatment provided that the full prescribed medication course was completed.

•Please contact the SSM Infectious Disease Division if you have further questions about this. Selecting 'unknown' will default to the most conservative course, which may not be appropriate depending on the patient's scenario.

6. Was the birthing parent adequately screened for syphilis during their pregnancy?
Adequate screening for syphilis during pregnancy is defined as at least two blood tests for syphilis, one which must have been obtained more than 4 weeks prior to delivery and another obtained within 4 weeks of delivery.
Within the SSM system, blood tests for syphilis include the treponema pallidum antibody (syphilis cascading antibody reflex; CMIA), RPR, and TP-PA. An RPR should have been obtained in cases of reactive CMIA.
